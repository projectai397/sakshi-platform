## Template Strategy

The outreach strategy employs three distinct approaches based on connection warmth and investor profile. Each template emphasizes different aspects of Sakshi's value proposition to align with specific investor theses.

**Template Categories:**
1. **Cold Outreach** - Direct emails to investors without prior connection
2. **Warm Introduction Request** - Emails requesting introductions from mutual connections
3. **Follow-Up Sequence** - Post-initial contact nurture emails

**Personalization Variables:**
- `[INVESTOR_NAME]` - Firm name (e.g., Omnivore Partners)
- `[PARTNER_NAME]` - Specific partner/investor name
- `[PORTFOLIO_COMPANY]` - Relevant portfolio company
- `[FOCUS_AREA]` - Investor's thesis alignment (e.g., agritech, wellness)
- `[YOUR_NAME]` - Founder name
- `[MUTUAL_CONNECTION]` - Shared connection name

## Email Best Practices

### Subject Line Guidelines

**Effective Patterns:**
- **Specificity:** "₹10 Cr raise for AI-powered wellness" (not "Investment opportunity")
- **Intrigue:** "400x more efficient than Dubai" (creates curiosity)
- **Credibility:** "10M+ community + 50K requests" (social proof)
- **Urgency:** "Closing in 4 weeks" (creates FOMO)

**Avoid:**
- Generic phrases ("Exciting opportunity", "Game-changer")
- All caps or excessive punctuation
- Misleading claims or clickbait
- Over-promising ("10x returns guaranteed")

### Email Body Guidelines

**Structure:**
1. **Hook (First 2 sentences):** Grab attention with compelling data or comparison
2. **Problem (1 paragraph):** Establish market pain point
3. **Solution (2-3 paragraphs):** Explain Sakshi's approach and differentiation
4. **Traction (Bullet points):** Prove validation and momentum
5. **Opportunity (1 paragraph):** Investment terms and returns
6. **Call-to-Action (1 sentence):** Clear next step

**Length:**
- Cold outreach: 250-350 words (2-3 minute read)
- Warm introduction: 150-200 words (1-2 minute read)
- Follow-ups: 100-150 words (under 1 minute)

**Tone:**
- Professional but personable
- Confident without arrogance
- Data-driven but not overwhelming
- Respectful of investor's time

### Personalization Checklist

**Before sending, verify:**
- [ ] Investor name and firm spelled correctly
- [ ] Specific portfolio company referenced
- [ ] Focus area matches their thesis
- [ ] Recent news or fund announcement mentioned (if applicable)
- [ ] Mutual connection confirmed (for warm intros)
- [ ] Links and attachments working
- [ ] Email sent from professional domain (not Gmail)
- [ ] Signature includes all contact methods

### Timing Strategy

**Best Times to Send:**
- **Day:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday (avoid Monday and Friday)
- **Time:** 8-10 AM or 2-4 PM (avoid lunch and end of day)
- **Avoid:** Holidays, long weekends, December (fundraising dead zone)

**Follow-Up Cadence:**
- Initial email: Day 0
- Follow-up 1: Day 3-4
- Follow-up 2: Day 10-12
- Follow-up 3 (final): Day 24-28
