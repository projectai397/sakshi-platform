## Negotiation Strategy

### Key Terms to Negotiate

**1. Valuation**
- **Target:** â‚¹50 crores post-money (â‚¹40 crores pre-money)
- **Range:** â‚¹40-60 crores post-money (willing to flex based on investor quality)
- **Rationale:** Comparable seed rounds for food-tech/wellness startups with similar traction

**2. Board Composition**
- **Target:** 3-person board (Founder, Lead Investor, Independent Director)
- **Range:** Willing to add second investor board seat if they bring significant strategic value
- **Rationale:** Maintain founder control while giving investors governance rights

**3. Liquidation Preference**
- **Target:** 1x non-participating preferred
- **Range:** Willing to accept 1x participating if valuation is favorable
- **Rationale:** Standard seed round terms, protects investors while aligning incentives

**4. Anti-Dilution Protection**
- **Target:** Broad-based weighted average
- **Range:** Not willing to accept full ratchet (too founder-unfriendly)
- **Rationale:** Standard seed round terms, protects investors from down rounds

**5. Pro-Rata Rights**
- **Target:** Pro-rata rights for all seed investors
- **Range:** Willing to give super pro-rata to lead investor
- **Rationale:** Allows investors to maintain ownership in future rounds

**6. Information Rights**
- **Target:** Quarterly financials and annual audited statements
- **Range:** Willing to provide monthly financials if investors request
- **Rationale:** Transparency builds trust, not burdensome to provide

**7. Vesting**
- **Target:** No founder vesting (already built the product)
- **Range:** Willing to accept 1-year cliff if investors insist
- **Rationale:** Founder has already invested significant time and capital

### Negotiation Tactics

**Tactic 1: Create Competitive Dynamics**

Don't negotiate with one investor in isolation. Share that you have multiple term sheets and are evaluating options. This creates urgency and prevents low-ball offers.

**Example:** "We've received three term sheets and are comparing terms. We're looking for investors who bring strategic value beyond capital, not just the highest valuation."

**Tactic 2: Focus on Strategic Value**

Emphasize that you're choosing investors based on strategic fit, not just terms. This allows you to accept slightly lower valuations from preferred investors without losing face.

**Example:** "Your expertise in [area] and portfolio company introductions are worth more to us than a 10% higher valuation from a purely financial investor."

**Tactic 3: Bundle Terms**

Don't negotiate terms one by one. Bundle them together so you can trade off (e.g., accept lower valuation in exchange for better board composition).

**Example:** "We're willing to accept â‚¹45 crore post-money if we can maintain a 3-person board with only one investor seat."

**Tactic 4: Use "Market Standard" Language**

Frame your positions as "market standard" rather than personal preferences. This makes them harder to negotiate away.

**Example:** "1x non-participating liquidation preference is market standard for seed rounds. We're not comfortable with participating preferred as it misaligns incentives."

**Tactic 5: Know Your Walk-Away Point**

Decide in advance what terms are non-negotiable. Be willing to walk away if investors push too hard.

**Example:** "We need to maintain at least 75% founder ownership post-seed to have meaningful equity for future rounds and team hires. Below that, we'd rather raise less capital or wait for better terms."
