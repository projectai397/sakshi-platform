# Museum of the Future - Al Waha (The Oasis) Research

## Executive Summary

Al Waha ("The Oasis" in Arabic) is a revolutionary wellness sanctuary located on the third floor of the Museum of the Future in Dubai. It represents a **speculative vision of wellness in 2071**, designed as an antidote to the destructive habits of digital life. The 3,000 sq ft space encourages visitors to **disconnect from technology and reconnect with mind, body, and spirit** through movement, meditation, and sensory therapy.

---

## Key Concept

**Philosophy**: "All change comes from within"

**Vision**: Future of well-being as a physical, social, and spiritual ecology

**Approach**: Inspired by Arab healing traditions + cutting-edge technology + proven science

**Design**: Inclusive vision through the female gaze, where care, connection, and spirit heal humanity

---

## Core Features

### 1. MOVE - Sand Bath & Walking Meditation

**Technology**: Generative real-time projection mapping

**Experience**:
- Soft carpet resembling fine white sand
- Each step leaves an imprint
- Visual patterns respond to movement
- Creates illusion of walking through ocean of sand
- Rhythms mimic sand and wind interaction
- Promotes slow, mindful movement
- Curative properties of walking meditation + water therapy

**Purpose**: Restore bodies from sedentary future life

---

### 2. FEEL - Ultrasonic Therapy

**Technology**: Ultrasonic haptic technology (mid-air tactile meditation)

**Experience**:
- Sit on stool, place hands over humming copper plate
- Feel steady, delicate vibrations
- Vibrations travel up arms to neck
- First mid-air tactile meditation in the world
- Re-develops human sensitivity

**Purpose**: Restore sensitivity and feeling to hands and mind

**Problem Addressed**: Digital clicking, typing, swiping reduces feeling in digits and decreases brain regions involved in awareness, empathy, and self-other processing

---

### 3. GROUND - Frequency Therapy

**Technology**: Large-scale gongs excited through healing sub-frequencies

**Experience**:
- Stand between two large metal discs
- Invisible healing frequencies (7.83 Hz - optimal human frequency)
- Sub-frequencies restore balance
- Promotes grounding

**Purpose**: Restore body's elemental vibration

**Problem Addressed**: Electromagnetic fields from digital integration raise stress levels

---

### 4. CONNECT - Collective Voice Therapy

**Technology**: Generative starscape triggered by collective humming

**Experience**:
- 6 people sit at concave-shaped table
- Each person leans into microphone and hums together
- Joint effort creates digitized rocks coming together at center
- Choreography of sound, light, and smell
- Unlocks healing energy of togetherness

**Purpose**: Relieve loneliness and alienation

**Problem Addressed**: Technology creates isolation and loneliness

---

### 5. MEDITATE - Meditation Center

**Technology**: Ceiling projection + immersive audio

**Experience**:
- Circular room with comfortable seats
- Projector beams water ripples and waves on ceiling
- Droning sounds of ocean and sea life
- Dimly lit, ethereal atmosphere
- Visitors stay 20-30 minutes

**Purpose**: Enjoy the present moment, stillness, mindfulness

---

### 6. ENTRANCE - Olfactory Experience

**Technology**: Corian fountain with scent diffusion

**Experience**:
- Marble-like fountain emanates sweet, earthy scent
- Tribulus omanense (national flower of UAE)
- Acts as mental palate cleanser

**Purpose**: Transition from technology to wellness

---

## Design Elements

**Architecture**: Mauve-colored Moroccan Riad style

**Layout**: Arched passageway leading to central courtyard

**Capacity**: 4 adjoining therapy rooms, 3-6 people each

**Booking**: No prior booking required, drop-in basis

**Atmosphere**: Serene, calming, ethereal

**Color Palette**: Mauve, pink, soft earth tones

**Materials**: Corian, copper, soft textiles

---

## Visitor Experience Flow

1. **Entry** → Smell tribulus omanense at fountain (olfactory reset)
2. **Move** → Walk across sand bath (movement therapy)
3. **Choose Therapy** → Select from 4 rooms:
   - Ultrasonic (FEEL)
   - Grounding (GROUND)
   - Connections (CONNECT)
   - Meditation (MEDITATE)
4. **Rest** → Spend time in meditation center
5. **Exit** → Return to museum refreshed

**Average Time**: 30-45 minutes

## Key Insights for Sakshi

### What Makes Al Waha Unique

1. **Technology Paradox**: Uses advanced technology to disconnect from technology
2. **Sensory Focus**: Each therapy targets specific sense (touch, movement, sound, sight)
3. **Cultural Authenticity**: Rooted in Arab healing traditions
4. **Collective Healing**: Emphasis on connection and togetherness
5. **Female Gaze**: Designed through lens of care, not optimization
6. **Speculative Future**: Set in 2071, imagining future wellness
7. **No Booking Required**: Accessible, drop-in model
8. **Included in Admission**: Part of museum experience (Dh145 = ~₹3,300)

### Lessons for Sakshi Centers

1. **Use technology to enable disconnection**, not more connection
2. **Create multi-sensory experiences** that engage all 5 senses
3. **Root in Indian healing traditions** (Ayurveda, Yoga, Pranayama)
4. **Emphasize collective healing** (group meditation, circle practices)
5. **Make it accessible** (triple pricing, no booking required)
6. **Design for the future** while honoring ancient wisdom
7. **Focus on restoration** from digital life damage
8. **Create sanctuary spaces** that feel otherworldly
